205.

Knowingly issuing or signing a false certificate relating to any fact of

which such certificate is by law admissible in evidence.

Using as a true certificate one known to be false in a material point.

False statement made in any declaration which is by law receivable as

evidence.

Using as true any such declaration known to be false.

Causing disappearance of evidence of an offence committed, or giving

false information touching it to screen the offender, if punishable with

imprisonment for life or imprisonment for 10 years.

If punishable with less than 10 years, imprisonment.

Intentional omission to give information of an offence by a person legally

bound to inform.

Giving false information respecting an offence committed.

Secreting or destroying any document to prevent its production

as evidence.

False personation for the purpose of any act or proceeding in a suit or

criminal prosecution, or for becoming bail or security.
